User centered design - Group Six

Redesigning the ticketing system in Metro de Porto

The idea

Metro do Porto is looking for a solution to prevent passengers of the metro system using the metro without a validated ticket. Metro do Porto does not intend to include physical barriers that alter its design, but rather to adopt another technological solution that detects whether or not a passenger is using a valid ticket and alert the passenger, agents and the public in general, resulting in a deterrent effect. The solution is to be implemented in all 14 underground stations, so overground stations are discluded from the solution.


We a proposing a system which indicates whether a passenger is travelling without a card using several different technologies. The solution is to utilize pressure plates, RFID readers and cameras. The key idea of the solution is to place glass panels after card validation points which cover the width of the passageway leading to the metro entrance. Passengers with invalidated cards passing over the glass panel will leave a noticeable trace along the panel, alerting the passenger violating the rules, fellow passengers and personnel. The solution has the advantage of not disrupting passengers abiding to the rules and offering a non disruptive way of alerting if a passenger is entering the underground system without a validated ticket.
